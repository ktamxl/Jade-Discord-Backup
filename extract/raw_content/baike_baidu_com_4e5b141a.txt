It has strong disease resistance. Typical yield per mu is 425–450 kilograms.2Local VarietySimiao Rice is most famous for that produced in Zengcheng, Guangdong. Zengcheng's Simiao Rice has long been honored with the beautiful title "Jade in Rice". It is renowned both domestically and internationally for its slender and pointed grains, rich oil content, crystal-clear and pure white appearance, soft cooked grains, and fragrant and delicious taste.CharacteristicsAccording to the  Guangdong Province  Local Standard "Zengcheng Simiao Rice" issued by the Guangdong Provincial Bureau of Quality and Technical Supervision in 2005, the sensory indicators for Zengcheng Simiao Rice are as follows: the color is lustrous and pure white, and the aroma possesses the inherent natural fresh fragrance characteristic of the local Zengcheng Simiao Rice. The physicochemical indicators for Zengcheng Simiao Rice are: the length-to-width ratio of the grain type is ≥2:8, with a length ranging from 5.4 to 6.6 mm, a 1000-grain weight of ≤12.59 g, a gel consistency of ≥50.0 mm, an amylose content of 17.0–25.0%, a chalky grain rate of ≤10.0%, and a chalkiness degree of ≤3.0. This constitutes the current legal standard for Zengcheng Simiao Rice. Rice that meets this standard qualifies as Zengcheng Simiao Rice; otherwise, it cannot be designated as such.Historical Origins"Zengcheng Simiao Rice" has a history of over 200 years. The 1820 edition of the Zengcheng County Gazetteer from the Qing Dynasty already documented Zengcheng Simiao Rice: "According to records, for early-ripening varieties, there are Dongchi and Shangzao Simiao; Baiguzai is quite good. For late-ripening varieties, there is Quanshui Zhan, with Simiao being the best." This indicates that "Simiao" was already the finest rice variety at that time. The authentic variety of Simiao is the late season type, commonly referred to as Simiao or Tall Stem Simiao Rice, and externally known as Zengcheng Simiao Rice. It belongs to a traditional farmer variety, specifically a photosensitive type, late season, high-quality variety.Place of OriginZengchengIn Danqiu Village, Zhucun Town (now renamed Zhucun Subdistrict), Zengcheng City, there is an area of terraced fields at Baishui Ca (local name), covering approximately 0.133 hectares (about 2 mu), divided into more than ten plots. The author has investigated the site and found it to be sandy, shallow-rooted farmland. Legend has it that this is the original birthplace of "Simiao." The legend dates back to the JiaQing period of the  Qing Dynasty .LegendOne day, two immortals (possibly Taoist priests) were traveling the world and arrived above Baishui Ca. Suddenly, a purple aura rose. Seeing the beautiful mountains and clear waters, the immortals believed that the land beneath the auspicious clouds and purple aura must be a Feng Shui Treasure Land. They descended their clouds, landed at Baishui Ca, established a temple, and sowed Rice seeds in these terraced fields. This variety of rice had golden husks, slender and long grains, a crystal-clear white color, and the rice emitted a silky luster. When cooked, the rice was smooth, refreshing, and delicious. Local farmers introduced and expanded the cultivation of this variety.Product CharacteristicsOriginally, this rice variety had no name. Based on its slender, long grains and silky luster, it was named "Simiao Rice" (literally "Silk Seedling Rice"). Consequently, word spread from ten to a hundred people, and the Simiao rice seeds quickly disseminated to various areas of Zengcheng County and were passed down from generation to generation. This is what later became known as "Tall Stem Simiao Rice." Typically, the stalks are around 120 centimeters tall, and in fertile fields, they can reach up to 136 centimeters.OriginSimiao RiceThe Zengcheng "Tall Stem Simiao Rice" evolved from wild rice in Zengcheng. This conclusion was reached after over 30 years of investigation, field study, and research.Main Basis1. The main morphology and characteristics of Zengcheng wild rice (belonging to common wild rice) are fundamentally similar to those of Tall Stem Simiao Rice, such as tall stems, narrow and long leaves, verdant leaf color, slender and slim grains, a yellowish-brown husk (lemma color), and a sickle-shaped awn tip on the lemma. However, "Simiao" is significantly more evolved than wild rice, with longer panicles, more grains per panicle, and a 1000-grain weight of 17.5-18 grams. The 1000-grain weight of wild rice is lighter (14.4 grams) because the grains are generally not plump.2. The place of origin of Zengcheng wild rice is close to that of Tall Stem Simiao Rice. It is widely acknowledged that the place of origin of Simiao rice is Baishui Ca, Danqiu Village, Zhucun Subdistrict. The place of origin of Zengcheng wild rice is the Yangtian River in Zhenlong Town, Zengcheng City (Luogang District), Guangdong Province. The Yangtian River flows into the Xifu River, and the Xifu River flows into the Dong River. According to a general survey in 1980, lush wild rice grew naturally along the swampy banks of the Yangtian River all the way to the Xifu River, covering a stretch of over 30 kilometers. Many were still growing there until the 1980s. According to expert investigation, Zengcheng wild rice has a history of over 9,000 years, having grown naturally here for a long time. The Xifu River flows from Zhenlong through Zhongxin and Zhucun, very close to the place of origin of "Simiao". The geographical locations are proximate, and the natural climatic conditions are identical.3. Tall Stem Simiao Rice is a late-season traditional farmer's Variety Type with strong photosensitivity. According to planting records from 1978, it was sown on June 16, transplanted on August 2, began heading on September 28, reached full heading on October 1, and matured on November 5. The acceptance results showed a total area of 3.17 mu, with a total yield of 612 kilograms and an average yield per mu of 193 kilograms. The heading period of Zengcheng wild rice is from September 25 to October 20, with the peak flowering period around October 10. Both are highly photosensitive and do not head in the early season. This shows that the heading period of Tall Stem Simiao Rice coincides with the early-maturing type of Zengcheng wild rice.From this, it can be inferred that Tall Stem Simiao Rice evolved over a long period from Zengcheng wild rice through natural derivation. As for the reason for the evolution of the wild rice, it might be due to natural cross-pollination between wild rice and cultivated rice causing variation, as rice paddies were adjacent to the wild rice; or it could be due to a genetic mutation in the wild rice, which was then selectively cultivated by local farmers or the Taoist priests at Baishui Ca. The farmers of Zengcheng were diligent and intelligent; when they discovered high-quality panicles resulting from natural variation in the wild rice, they would pick them and bring them back for sowing. This was the very first "Simiao". Although the yield of Tall Stem Simiao Rice was not high, it continued to be used until 1973 due to its exceptionally high rice quality and excellent taste.ApproachAfter 1974, the old Simiao rice varieties had essentially exited the historical stage due to long-term degradation, tall stems prone to lodging, and excessively low yields. To restore and develop the famous local specialty Zengcheng Simiao Rice, experts began persistent efforts from 1972 to cultivate a new generation of high-yield, high-quality, and high-efficiency Simiao rice varieties. Their diligent and intelligent work led to the continuous emergence of new Simiao rice varieties, fostering a vigorous development in Simiao rice production. Based on the experts' over 30 years (1972-2005) of practice in Simiao rice genetic breeding, the following describes the breeding approaches and technical routes for the new generation of "Simiao Rice".Systematic Selection MethodTo improve and dwarf the Tall Stem Simiao Rice, systematic selection breeding was employed starting in 1972. Individual plants from varieties like Tall Stem Simiao Rice were selected through systematic methods, leading to the cultivation of several high-quality, high-yield, and high-efficiency varieties such as the special superior variety Simiao Selection No. 6, White Simiao No. 3, Guoqing Zao Zhan, and Shuang'er Zao Zhan. Among them, the famous special superior variety Simiao Selection No. 6, developed in 1978, became the pioneer of the new generation Simiao rice. It increased yield by approximately 20% on average compared to Tall Stem Simiao Rice, playing a significant role in restoring and developing Zengcheng Simiao Rice production.Biparental Hybridization Breeding MethodStarting in 1974, hybridization breeding methods were formally used to cultivate dwarf, lodging-resistant, special superior, and high-yield Simiao rice varieties. Many hybrid combinations were made in the 1970s. The most effective and representative one utilized the cross between Tall Stem Simiao Rice and the dwarf, special superior variety Shuangzhu Zhan. In 1985, this led to the development of "Double Simiao No. 2", a new generation dwarf Simiao variety with lodging resistance, superior rice quality, and an approximate 80% yield increase over Tall Stem Simiao Rice. Beginning in 1980, a different approach was taken by directly utilizing distant hybridization breeding between Zengcheng wild rice and cultivated rice. This resulted in the famous high-quality, high-yield "Guiye Zhan" series varieties and "Guiye Wan Zhan". Concurrently, numerous high-quality, disease-resistant materials (lines, strains) from the progeny of wild and cultivated hybrids were accumulated, laying the foundation for utilizing wild rice to breed special superior Simiao rice varieties.Simiao RiceWild Rice Multiple Cross HybridizationIn order to cultivate Simiao Rice varieties with significant breakthroughs, characterized by wide adaptability, exceptional quality, high yield, and high efficiency, a multi-parent cross-breeding and genetic improvement program utilizing Zengcheng wild rice and excellent cultivated rice germplasm from both domestic and international sources was initiated in 1980. This effort has resulted in the development of over 30 distinct, high-quality, high-yield, and high-efficiency "Simiao Rice" varieties with broad adaptability, promoting the development of Simiao Rice. Now, the general public can enjoy fragrant, smooth, and delicious Simiao rice throughout the year. Several representative examples are provided below to illustrate that utilizing wild rice in multi-parent cross-breeding for developing Simiao varieties is a successful and viable approach.Example one: Wild Australian Simiao. The hybridization combination involved Guichao No. 2, Zengcheng wild rice, and Australian Kangaroo Simiao. Through the optimal combination of these three distantly related parents, each with distinct characteristics, the high-yield potential and broad adaptability of Guichao No. 2, the robust vitality and high resistance to multiple pests and diseases of the wild rice, and the excellent grain quality and flavor of Australian Kangaroo Simiao were successfully integrated into the "Wild Australian Simiao." Many traits even exhibited significant transgressive segregation: it demonstrated wide adaptability and good high-yield performance, suitable for cultivation in vast areas south of the Yangtze River, and could be used for both the early and late seasons in South China. It generally yielded around 450 kilograms per mu (approximately equivalent to 0.067 hectares), with high-yield fields exceeding 550 kilograms per mu. Provincial quality inspection rated its grain quality as special grade one, superior for export. These achievements represented major breakthroughs for "Simiao" varieties, as the old Simiao varieties had very narrow adaptability and low yields. Their historical highest yield per mu never reached 250 kilograms, with typical yields only between 150-200 kilograms per mu. In their original production area, Zengcheng City, they were also limited to late-season planting and could not head and set seed when moved north of Shaoguan.Example two: Guiye Simiao. The hybridization combination involved Guichao No. 2, Zengcheng wild rice // Tall Stem Simiao. Guiye Simiao combined the main advantages of its three parent lines, particularly in grain shape, grain quality, flavor, and even hull color, which were very similar to those of Tall Stem Simiao. Its grain quality was rated special grade one, superior for export. It exhibited broad adaptability, short stature with lodging resistance, suitability for both early and late seasons, and high yield and efficiency. Several superior strains of Guiye Simiao each had their own characteristics; apart from sharing similar grain quality and flavor, they differed in growth period and plant morphology. Among them, Guiye Simiao No. 1 and No. 3 were medium-maturing in the early season, No. 6 was medium-late maturing, and No. 2 and No. 22 were early-maturing.Example three, Zengye Simiao. The parent combination is: Guichao No. 2 / Zengcheng wild rice // Tall Stem Simiao Rice /// Australian Kangaroo Simiao. Besides inheriting the advantages of Guichao No. 2, such as suitability for both the early and late seasons, wide adaptability, high and stable yield, Zengye Simiao successfully combines the attractive grain shape and unique quality flavor of Tall Stem Simiao Rice with the superior rice quality and flavor of the internationally renowned Australian Kangaroo Simiao from the global rice market. It exhibits exceptionally high rice quality, with slender and slim grains that are crystal clear and attractive. The cooked rice is soft, smooth, and delicious. Its quality and nutritional components are of a higher grade than the traditional Zengcheng Simiao Rice. It also features a high milling recovery rate and is rich in nutritional components. According to measurements by  South China Agricultural University  and the  Guangdong Academy of Agricultural Sciences , its brown rice rate is 79.2%, its milled rice rate is 71.3%, its protein content is 10.48%, and its content of 17 amino acids is 7.18%. It is rich in Vitamin A and B. Notably, it also contains Vitamin E and mineral nutrients beneficial for human health and disease prevention, such as zinc (26.3 mg/kg), selenium (0.102 mg/kg), iron (47.2 mg/kg), manganese (45.0 mg/kg), and copper (1.83 mg/kg). It is known as "high-quality nutritious and health-promoting rice." Zengye Simiao nutritious and health-promoting rice won the International Gold Award at the Second Asia International Fair of Famous, Excellent, New Products, Technologies, and Inventions in February 1999.Example four, Red Wild Simiao No. 6. Therefore, Red Wild Simiao No. 6 has a very rich genetic foundation. The most significant characteristic of Red Wild Simiao is that the Simiao Rice grains contain natural shrimp-like pigment, presenting a shrimp-red color, hence it is called red rice Simiao or red Simiao rice. This variety of Simiao Rice is created by using computers to precisely calculate its blending ratio, achieved through repeated trials.The production of one bag of Simiao Rice requires going through the following 12 processes:(1) Raw Material Origin: Uses high-quality Simiao Rice originally produced in Zengcheng.(2) Grain Selection: Specialized personnel inspect and accept the grains into the warehouse, ensuring the grain quality meets requirements.(3) Removal of Impurities: Utilizes magnetism to remove iron powder and nails mixed into the grains.(4) Stone Removal: A stone remover machine eliminates impurities like stones, ensuring the paddy rice contains no foreign components.(5) Husking: Uses advanced machinery to remove the husk from the grain without breaking the rice.(6) Brown Rice and Refined Rice Selection: Rice quality inspection is conducted to ensure the quality of the rice grains.(7) Whitening: After the brown rice is separated from the husk and selected, it enters the second stage of whitening.(8) White rice selection: Further select the white rice to ensure the rice grains meet the requirements.(9) Laser color sorting: Use a color sorter to remove moldy rice and other impurities.(10) Polished rice polishing: Use a polishing machine to increase the whiteness of the rice.(11) Polished rice inspection: Conduct a final inspection of the quality and weight of each bag of rice to ensure there is absolutely no discrepancy.(12) Packaging: When packaging the rice, remove the air from the bags to keep it fresh for a long time and ensure the quality remains unchanged over time.Breeding BaseSimiao RiceThe Zengcheng City High-Quality Rice Breeding Base is located along the Licheng Tang Village section of the Zengpai Highway. This shows that the cultivation of Simiao Rice in this city has a history of over 100 years.By the early period after the founding of the People's Republic of China, Simiao Rice was still cultivated over a relatively large area in the city. According to oral accounts: in Zheng'guo, Simiao late rice accounted for four or five-tenths of the planting area, and a significant amount of Simiao grain was used to pay the agricultural tax. This indicates the wide extent of Simiao cultivation at the time.From the founding of the PRC to the mid-to-late 1950s, due to the long cultivation history of traditional old Simiao varieties, severe varietal mixing and degeneration occurred, manifesting in low yield, susceptibility to diseases, and poor lodging resistance. Furthermore, grain production at the time emphasized high yield and self-sufficiency, neglecting quality, and prices were not determined based on quality. Consequently, the planting area of Simiao Rice decreased year by year. The famous Short Stem Simiao Rice became extinct in 1958. By 1973, the planting area of Tall Stem Simiao Rice in the entire county was less than 50,000 mu (approximately 3,333 hectares), with a total output of 9 million kilograms and an export volume of 5 million kilograms. By 1982, the planting area in the entire county was less than two hundred mu (approximately 13.3 hectares).Agricultural departments had long been concerned about the varietal mixing and the shrinking planting area of Simiao Rice. Since the 1980s, through persistent efforts by agricultural scientists and technicians from the city's Agricultural Bureau Seed Station and the Municipal Agricultural Science Research Institute, employing techniques such as systematic selection, distant hybridization and genetic breeding with wild rice, and focused solar radiation mutagenesis breeding, they successfully bred a series of new Simiao varieties (lines), including Simiao Selection No. 6, Short Simiao 83-1, Short Simiao Selection No. 8, Wild Australian Simiao, Guiye Simiao, Zengye Simiao, and Long Grain Simiao. Their common characteristics are: the Taste and eating quality are indistinguishable from the original old Simiao varieties, while the yield is several times higher than that of the original old Simiao varieties, with significantly enhanced resistance to pests and diseases and lodging, and broader adaptability. Therefore, they were rapidly adopted for widespread use in production. The new Simiao varieties were not only planted extensively in Zengcheng City but were also introduced in large quantities to neighboring provinces, cities, and counties.The China Grain Industry Association has honored  Zengcheng District  in Guangdong Province as the "Hometown of Chinese Simiao Rice". Simiao Rice is most famous for being produced in Zengcheng, Guangdong. Zengcheng's Simiao Rice has long been praised as the "Jade in Rice", renowned both domestically and internationally for its slender grains, rich oil content, crystal-clear whiteness, soft cooked rice, and fragrant, delicious Taste.1The ancient local farmer varieties of Simiao have been replaced by newly bred varieties. The preserved individual plants are retained solely as breeding materials for future generations.Simiao Selection No. 6, Short Simiao 83-1, and Short Simiao Selection No. 8 were the main cultivated Simiao varieties in Zengcheng City during the 1980s and are no longer used in production today. Wild Australian Simiao, Guiye Simiao, and Zengye Simiao were the main cultivated Simiao varieties in Zengcheng City during the 1990s and have now been replaced by new Simiao varieties such as Long Grain Simiao.Honors and AwardsIn 1986, Zengcheng was approved by the National Ministry of Agriculture as a high-quality rice base county.In 1994, the Zengcheng Simiao Rice comprehensive development project was included in the national "Spark Program".In 1995, it was recognized by the National Ministry of Agriculture as a commercial grain base county.In 1995, Zengcheng Fragrant Simiao Rice won the Gold Award at the China Agricultural Expo.In 1999, Zengcheng Wild Simiao Nutritious Health Rice and Red Wild Simiao Nutritious Health Rice won the "International Gold Award" and the "International Special Honor Gold Award", respectively.In 2007, the rice cultivation area for Zengcheng Simiao Rice was over 32,000 hectares, with the output of high-quality Zengcheng Simiao Rice exceeding 180,000 tons and the output value reaching nearly 400 million yuan.
