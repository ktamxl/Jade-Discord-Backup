### ⭐ 譚遠詒 (Tam Yuen Yi) — THE STAR
**Role:** Ken's grandfather. 遠 generation.
**Arrived:** ~1900–1920, age 16, ALONE. Paper son of 阿澤.
**Education:** None.

**What he built:**
- Laundry business in San Francisco
- Store selling goods on **Grant Avenue, Chinatown**
- Co-founder of **譚家公所 (Tam Family Association)** — building still stands in Chinatown, owns properties, collects rent, still active

**The basket of gold coins:**
One of the most vivid scenes in this story: 遠詒 kept a basket of gold coins in his store. When his nephew 卓賢 came asking for gambling money, 遠詒 would reach into the basket and throw him one. Casually. Without argument. This image speaks volumes — a man who arrived penniless at 16, built enough wealth that gold coins were kept in a basket, and distributed with the calm authority of someone who could afford to be generous even with the undeserving.

**His authority:**
- Commanded his uncle 阿澤 (his senior by hierarchy) to stay in San Diego, away from SF gambling — and 阿澤 obeyed
- Dealt with nephew 卓賢's gambling habit with quiet, resigned generosity
- Respected so deeply that decades after his death, his son was introduced as "遠詒's son" and rooms changed energy

**Theory (Ken's):** 遠詒 likely helped other paper sons navigate immigration — coaching papers, Angel Island interrogations. His legendary status in Chinatown suggests debts owed by many families, carried silently for generations. (The chairs behind the wife's back. The receipt story. All whispers of old obligations.)

**What happened after his death:** 🔴 MYSTERY. 南伯 implied 卓賢 did something. Details withheld at the time to protect the newly arrived family. TO BE REVEALED.

**這句話揭示的新資訊：**

**歷史的殘酷：**
遠詒的承諾從未兌現。他的兒子們在廣東等他，卻等來了毛澤東。

### 阿澤 — THE CAUTIONARY UNCLE
**Role:** 遠詒's uncle (senior by hierarchy). Father of Ken's uncle (side branch). NOT 遠詒's father.
**Character:** Gambler. Not respected in family. Low status in conversations.
**Fate:** Commanded by 遠詒 to remain in San Diego, away from SF gambling scene.
**Note:** Possibly the paper "father" through whom 遠詒 entered the US.

### 譚卓賢 "Chuck" — THE COMPLICATED ONE 🔴
**Role:** Ken's uncle. Likely son of 阿澤 or related branch. 定 generation (昻初's cousin/uncle).
**English name:** Chuck
**Character:**
- Gambler (like 阿澤 — the pattern continues)
- "Useless, never really held a job" — described by 南伯
- Would go to 遠詒's store and ask for gambling money; 遠詒 would throw him a gold coin from the basket
- Despite being "useless," had enough standing/connections to sponsor 譚昻初 to the US

**His role in the family's 1972 arrival:**
- Sponsored 譚昻初 (responsible for his employment) to come to the US
- Housed the family in his **basement** when they first arrived
- After a few weeks, moved them to a **one-bedroom Chinatown apartment** (rat and roach infested — Ken grew up there)
- On moving day: handed 譚昻初 a paper bag with a carton of milk and small groceries
- **Then handed him the RECEIPT** — to be collected when 譚昻初 finds a job

**The receipt scene:** This is the most revealing moment of 卓賢's character. In the act of apparent generosity — helping a cousin's family to a new home — he handed over a bill for a carton of milk. He kept score even in the smallest moments. What does that suggest about larger matters? What happened after 遠詒 died?

**The mystery:** 南伯 implied 卓賢 did something after 遠詒's death. 南伯 chose not to reveal it at the time — the family was new, vulnerable, still under 卓賢's roof. This secret has never been fully told. 🔴

### 南伯 — THE KEEPER OF TRUTH
**Role:** Uncle/elder cousin to 譚昻初 (Ken calls him 南伯 — respected elder). Nephew of 遠詒. Grew up alongside 卓賢.
**Character:** Kind, unpretentious, a good man. Not a legend — but decent and loyal.
**Personal life:**
- Divorced. Wife left him for a Caucasian man; she lived in a **trailer park in Reno, Nevada**
- 南伯 himself lived in **Reno**, working as a **Keno worker at a Casino**
- Not wealthy — just making a living
- Visited Ken's family often in SF. A caring presence.

**The 南伯 / 卓賢 dynamic:**
- Both nephews of 遠詒. About the same age. Grew up together.
- They knew each other's full story — every secret.
- 南伯's tone when speaking of 卓賢 was heavy with something unspoken.
- He clearly wanted to tell 譚昻初 the full truth — but held back. The family was new, vulnerable, still needing 卓賢.
- This silence is one of the great mysteries of this story. 🔴

**南伯's children:**

#### Stella (daughter)
- Late teens when Ken met her in 1972 → born ~1953–1955
- Very kind — drove Ken's family to Reno to visit 南伯; also took them to the trailer park to meet his ex-wife
- Married a **Chinese man** who worked as an **engineer at Bechtel**
- Family lived **abroad for many years — Middle East (possibly Saudi Arabia or UAE)**, likely building oil refineries, until husband retired
- That's why contact was lost for long stretches
- Now living in **Alameda** (East Bay, SF area)
- Has children (number unknown to Ken)
- Ken and Stella meet occasionally at family weddings and funerals
- Ken hoped to see her at 譚昻初's funeral (March 2026)

#### Johnny (son)
- ~5 years older than Ken → born ~1958
- Very much like his father 南伯: quiet, reserved, not social
- "Sort of like a hermit" — Ken's words
- **Financial planner**, lives alone in **Marina District, San Francisco**
- Still renting. No partner.
- Ken tried inviting him to lunch — he wasn't interested. Ken even tried to set him up with girls in younger years (no luck).
- Not gay (Ken's read) — just deeply reserved
- Carries the same quiet, self-contained energy as 南伯
- Lives his life on his own terms, privately

**Ken's note on 南伯's family:** "That wraps up 南伯's family."

### 譚昻初 (Tam Ngong Chor) — THE LOST GENERATION
**Role:** Ken's father. 定 generation.
**Born:** 1936-07-14, Guangdong (not born in America)
**Passed:** 2026-03-08
**In America:** Arrived 1972. Volunteered as accountant for 譚家公所 for many years after retirement. Stopped when he got older.

#### 遠詒的三個兒子（✅ 2026-03-14 新確認）
- 遠詒共有三子，全部留在廣東讀書
- **長子（大哥）**：聰明靈活，圓滑但不老實。曾騙了人，對方找上門來。他是生意的精明操盤手，但錢財方面不可信。
- **次子（中子）= 譚昻初**：本故事的中心人物。老實、勤勞，學了釀酒手藝，是做事的人，不是操控人的人。
- **幼子（細佬）**：媽媽最疼愛的孩子。曾在船上工作，走遍海外，有見識，生活尚可但不富裕。大多數時間不在國內，暫不是故事重心。

#### 香港歲月 — 酒舖與兄弟的恩怨
昻初在香港期間，拿出所有積蓄與**長兄合夥**開了一間**中國米酒及洋酒舖**：
- 昻初負責：學習並掌握**釀造中國米酒的技術**（做事的人）
- 長兄負責：引入**西方品牌洋酒**銷售，也**掌管所有財務**（操控的人）
- 生意本來很好
- 但財務由長兄一手掌控，最終**生意倒閉**（原因不詳——長兄的操守成疑）

**倒閉後：**
- 昻初希望**借用長兄的酒牌（liquor license）**另起爐灶
- 長兄**拒絕**
- 昻初最終為人打工，替老闆釀酒，眼看老闆賺錢
- 但他心裡已在盤算移民，所以也不太在意

#### 南伯母親的房子（方柳燕轉述）
- 昻初和方柳燕在香港期間，**住在南伯母親的家，並負責照顧她**
- 南伯早已身在美國（以遠詒紙兒子身份），需要人照顧老母
- 昻初夫婦默默承擔了這責任，暗暗希望老人百年歸老後，房子（公寓）會作為回報留給他們
- **結果：沒有發生。** 昻初夫婦大概自己付了租金。又一次付出而沒有回報。

#### 昻初在美國的夢想——與Ken的對話（關鍵場景）
有一次Ken與父母昻初、柳燕共進午飯，父親訴說了在美國的遭遇：
- 他曾想做生意，找兄弟朋友合夥，無人感興趣
- 他最大的夢想是**開一間製衣廠（garment factory）**

**Ken當時直接說出的話（偏印格性格）：**
> 「你當年沒有人支持你開廠，其實是幸運的。那些錢最後用來買了房子，才是正確的。」
> 「製衣廠那個年代已經走下坡，廠主都急著脫手，因為工序都去了香港，之後又去了中國大陸。」
> 「你英文不好，沒有生意經驗，只懂得做，不懂得管，最終一定失敗。」
> 「你想借酒牌開店，為什麼你不自己去研究怎樣申請一張酒牌？沒有牌照為什麼就停了下來？」

#### Ken對「失落的一代」的定義（✅ 2026-03-14 正式記錄）
> *「他們被賣了一個金山夢（Gold Mountain dream）。沒有教育，沒有技能，只有一個夢。等我輪到去，我就會發達。那就是失落的一代——no education, no skills, just a dream。」*

這一代人：
- 靠互相依賴生存（rely on each other to survive）
- 不具備自己學習和解決問題的能力
- 把希望寄託在機遇和他人身上，而非自己的能力
- 被「金山」神話催眠，以為到了就會成功

#### ⭐ 最重要的一句話（Ken親口，2026-03-14）
Sisi的分析：「父輩的期望，化作無形的重量，一直在鞭策你。」

Ken的回應：
> **"I BECAME THE DREAM."**

昻初這一生未能實現的夢，未能走過的路——Ken接過來了。
不靠等待，不靠依賴，靠自己一步一步走。

## Preston 譚詠昌 — The White Socks Story

Ken shared this story after reading the BaZi line: *"The strength that makes you principled can make you immovable when you shouldn't be. Even mountains are carved by rivers over time."*

When Preston was about 6 or 7 years old, he would **only wear white socks**. Any socks with stripes or color, he simply would not wear.

One morning, running late for school, Ken and Mabel discovered no white socks had been laundered. They asked Preston to just wear the colored ones. He refused. They were desperate. Ken forced the colored socks onto him — and Preston immediately pulled them off.

Ken got so angry he hit Preston. **It was the first time he ever hit him. And the last.**

*"After that, my heart was hurting more than his butt."*

**Ken's reflection:** That line in the BaZi report — about inflexibility — made him literally drop when he read it. The mountain child, at age 6, already fully formed. 戊土日主 — the socks confirmed it thirty years before anyone read a chart.


**Ken highlighted that line with a yellow marker before showing Preston the report.** 🖊️
