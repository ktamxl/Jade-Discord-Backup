# Shallot Oil Chicken & Noodles (蔥油雞腿)

**Cuisine:** Chinese (Cantonese / Shanghai fusion)
**Serves:** 2–4
**Prep Time:** 30 min soak + 2 hrs marinate (ideally overnight)
**Cook Time:** ~1 hour active
**Source:** https://youtu.be/1fsoQMjiBSU

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken
| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Chicken legs | 800g (~2 large) |
| Salt | 2 tsp |
| Ginger slices | 8 pieces |
| Green onion (shallot) | 1 stalk, cut into sections |
| Shaoxing wine | 2 tbsp |
| Garlic powder (or sand ginger powder) | ½ tbsp |

### Shallot Oil
| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Green onions | 100g |
| Dried shallot | 1 piece |
| Red onion | ½ piece |
| Coriander (with root) | 1 stalk |
| Ginger | 3 slices |
| Cooking oil | ~200ml (2× weight of solids) |

> 💡 **Ratio rule:** 1 part aromatics : 2 parts oil by weight

### Secret Shallot Soy Sauce
| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Steamed chicken juice (from steaming plate) | 3 tbsp |
| Sugar | 4 tsp |
| Light soy sauce | 3 tbsp |
| Oyster sauce | 1.5 tbsp |
| Prepared shallot oil | 6 tbsp |
| Dark soy sauce | A few drops (optional — for noodles, deeper color) |

### Garnish & Sides
- Green onion shreds, cucumber shreds, or julienned ginger
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Noodles (udon, Shanghai noodles, or any type)
- Fried egg (1 per serving)

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Prep & Soak the Chicken
1. Clean chicken legs thoroughly.
2. Soak in lightly salted water for **30 minutes** to remove any blood and odor.
3. Drain and pat **completely dry** with paper towels.

### Step 2 — Marinate
1. Rub salt evenly over all surfaces of the chicken.
2. Add ginger slices, green onion sections, garlic powder, and Shaoxing wine.
3. Massage aromatics into the meat well.
4. Cover and refrigerate for at least **2 hours** — overnight is best.

### Step 3 — Make the Shallot Oil
1. Lightly smash green onion heads; cut stalks into sections.
2. Slice red onion, dried shallot, and ginger. Cut coriander into segments.
3. Place all aromatics into a **cold pan** and add the oil.
4. Heat on **low heat** for **5–8 minutes**, stirring occasionally.
5. Once ingredients turn **golden brown** and steam stops rising (moisture is gone), turn off heat. Let residual heat finish browning — remove any pieces that go too dark (they turn bitter).
6. Strain oil into a sterilized glass jar. Reserve crispy fried green onion pieces for garnish.

> ⚠️ Always start cold oil + low heat. Never rush this step.

### Step 4 — Steam the Chicken
1. Place marinated chicken legs on a plate in a steamer.
2. Steam on **high heat** for **20 minutes**.
3. Turn off heat and let sit (residual steam) for **5 more minutes**.
4. Remove chicken, let cool. **Save all the juice** collected on the steaming plate — this is liquid gold.

### Step 5 — Make the Sauce
1. Combine **3 tbsp hot chicken juice** with sugar. Stir until dissolved.
2. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and 6 tbsp shallot oil.
3. Mix well. Taste and adjust — should be slightly sweet, savory, and fragrant.

### Step 6 — Shred & Serve the Chicken
1. Hand-shred cooled chicken, discarding bones.
2. Arrange on a plate. Top with fresh green onion shreds.
3. Pour sauce over chicken.
4. **Optional:** Flash-heat a spoonful of shallot oil until smoking; pour over the green onions for a burst of wok hei aroma.
5. Finish with toasted sesame seeds.

### Step 7 — Shallot Oil Noodles (Optional but essential)
1. Boil noodles per package instructions. Drain well.
2. Mix sauce in a bowl (add a few drops dark soy sauce for deeper color if desired).
3. Toss noodles in the sauce.
4. Top with a fried egg and reserved crispy fried shallots.

---

## Chef's Notes
- **Storage:** Shallot oil keeps in a cool dry place for up to 1 month.
- **Don't rush the oil:** Low-and-slow draws out all the aromatics without burning. Any scorched pieces = bitter oil.
- **Flavor balance:** Sauce is intentionally slightly sweet — adjust sugar to taste.
- **Noodle pairing:** Shanghai noodles or udon are excellent; thin rice vermicelli works too.
- **The chicken juice is the secret:** Don't discard it — it concentrates all the umami from the marinade + steam.
