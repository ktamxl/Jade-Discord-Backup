# Portuguese Curry Chicken

**Cuisine:** Portuguese-Chinese Fusion
**Serves:** 4
**Prep Time:** 30 min (+ overnight marinate)
**Cook Time:** 45 min + 20 min oven

---

## Marinade (Chicken)

| Ingredient | Qty |
|---|---|
| Salt | 1 tsp |
| Sugar | 1 tbsp |
| Onion Powder | 2 tsp |
| Garlic Powder | 1 tsp |
| Curry Powder | ½ tsp |

---

## Main Ingredients

| Ingredient | Qty | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chicken | 450 g | Cut into pieces |
| Onion | 200 g | ½ medium onion |
| Potato | 500 g | 4 medium, cut into chunks |
| Carrot | 2 sticks | Sliced |
| Bell Pepper | ½ | Any colour |
| Shallot | 1 small | Sliced |
| Shaved Coconut | 2 tbsp | For topping |

---

## Cooking Ingredients

### Roux
| Ingredient | Qty |
|---|---|
| Flour | 2 tbsp (50 g) |
| Unsalted Butter | 2 tbsp (½ stick) |

### Gravy Mixer
| Ingredient | Qty |
|---|---|
| Turmeric Powder | 1 tbsp |
| Curry Powder | ½ tsp |
| Chicken Stock | 1 can |
| Coconut Cream | ½ can |
| Water | ½ can (use stock can as measure) |

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate chicken** — combine all marinade ingredients with chicken, mix well, refrigerate overnight.

2. **Prep vegetables** — blanch carrots until just tender; pan-fry potatoes until golden on the outside. Set both aside.

3. **Make roux** — melt butter in a small pan over low heat, whisk in flour, cook 1–2 min until pale golden and smooth. Set aside.

4. **Mix gravy** — combine turmeric powder, curry powder, chicken stock, coconut cream, and water. Stir well.

5. **Pan-fry chicken** — lightly sear marinated chicken in a hot pan. Set aside (does not need to be fully cooked at this stage).

6. **Build the base** — add oil to pan, stir-fry shallots, onion, and bell pepper until softened.

7. **Add gravy** — pour in the gravy mixer. Bring to a simmer.

8. **Thicken** — add roux to the simmering gravy, lower heat, stir until smooth and creamy.

9. **Combine** — once gravy is creamy, add carrots, potatoes, and chicken. Stir gently to coat. Allow to return to a simmer.

10. **Oven finish** — transfer everything to an oven-safe pan. Sprinkle shaved coconut evenly over the top. Bake at **400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes**.

11. **Serve** — serve hot over steamed rice or with crusty bread.

---

## Notes

- The overnight marinate is important — the sugar helps caramelise and the spices penetrate the chicken deeply.
- Roux gives the gravy a silky, restaurant-style finish — don't skip it.
- Shaved coconut on top toasts in the oven and adds subtle sweetness and texture.
- Oven temp and time do **not** change when scaling — only ingredient quantities scale.
- Can substitute coconut milk for coconut cream for a lighter gravy.
